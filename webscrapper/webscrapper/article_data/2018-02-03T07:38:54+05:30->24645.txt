HYDEARABAD: Sleuth of special operation team, Malkajgiri zone, on Thursday arrested five persons for trying to exchanging demonetised currency and seized notes valued 99 lakh. The accused have been identified as   (48), B Tirupathi (35), Mohammed Rizwan-Ul-Hassan (36),   (48) and   (49). One more accused Imtiyaz is on the run. 

 According to police,   and Imtiyaz are neighbours in   and are into real estate business. Through their business, both the accused procured demonetised notes of 1,000 and 500 from known persons. 
 
 The duo had planned to exchange the demonetised currency with a commission of 20 per cent and also contacted other associates to find such customers. The special operation team officials received information about their plan and they conducted a decoy operation at Prashant Nagar in Uppal.
  
  
 
 When the accused persons came with a bag filled with demonetised notes to exchange, police caught them red handed. Cops seized 1,000 and 500 notes valued 99 lakh along with seven mobile phones and a car from their possession. 
 
 Police registered a case and booked the accused under Section 420 (cheating), 511 (Punishment for attempting to commit offences punishable with imprisonment for life or other imprisonment) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and Section 7 of Specified Bank Notes (Cessation of Liabilities) Act, 2017.
