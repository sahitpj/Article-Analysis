 TIMES VIEW
 
 That the average traffic speed in Bengaluru has more than halved in just eight years, reveals two factors bedevilling road users in the past decade: An exponential increase in the number of new vehicles and the lack of preparedness on the part of the government to remove bottlenecks as soon as gridlocks start surfacing. High traffic density is the bane of every big city, and Bengaluru is getting a taste of this urban malady only in recent years. The best way out of the traffic mess is to integrate all modes of transport and make urban commuting seamless. The bigger problem -an ever-burgeoning number of new vehicles being added to the city's roads -could be tackled by widening carriageways within the given grid and having smaller and effective flyovers.
